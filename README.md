# Instacart-Market-Basket-Analysis

# Description

Instacart is a grocery delivery platform that allows customers to order groceries online and have them delivered, similar to services like Uber Eats or DoorDash. This dataset captures detailed information about customer shopping behavior, including items purchased, order sequences, and product categories. It is useful for analyzing purchase patterns, popular products, and cart-building behaviors.

The dataset contains individual-level entries with details such as product name, category, order sequence, quantity, and add-to-cart priority, enabling insights into which products are added first and overall shopping trends.

## Technologies

Pandas – data manipulation

NumPy – numerical computing

Matplotlib – visualization

Seaborn – statistical plotting

## Dataset

The dataset used in this project is publicly available on Kaggle:https://www.kaggle.com/c/instacart-market-basket-analysis/overview

## Key Insights

- Orders are concentrated during daytime hours, especially between 10:00 and 16:00, while nighttime activity remains low.

- High reorder rate indicates that customers frequently repurchase previously selected products.

- Most users exhibit repeat purchasing behavior, suggesting consistent and loyal shopping patterns.

- Medium-sized baskets are most common, while very large orders are relatively rare.

- Certain products consistently rank among the most frequently ordered items, representing essential daily goods.

- Products added earlier to the cart often reflect priority or staple items.

## Conclusion

This exploratory data analysis highlights clear patterns in customer shopping behavior on Instacart. The findings show strong evidence of repeat purchasing habits, daytime shopping preferences, and essential-product dominance in grocery baskets.
